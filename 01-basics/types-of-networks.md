# Types of Networks

Networks are classified based on their size, coverage area, and purpose.


## 1. PAN (Personal Area Network)
- Covers a very small area
- Used for personal devices

**Examples:**
- Bluetooth between mobile and earphones
- Hotspot between phone and laptop

**Security Note:**
- Vulnerable to unauthorized Bluetooth access

---

## 2. LAN (Local Area Network)
- Covers a small geographic area like a room, building, or campus
- High speed and privately owned

**Examples:**
- Home Wi-Fi network
- College computer lab

**Security Note:**
- Attacks like ARP spoofing and internal scanning are common

---

## 3. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
- Covers a city or large campus
- Larger than LAN but smaller than WAN

**Examples:**
- City-wide internet services
- University networks


## 4. WAN (Wide Area Network)
- Covers large geographical areas (countries or continents)
- Uses public or private communication links

**Examples:**
- The Internet
- Corporate branch networks

**Security Note:**
- Targeted by DDoS and routing attacks


## Summary
| Network Type | Coverage Area | Example |
|-------------|---------------|--------|
| PAN | Personal | Bluetooth |
| LAN | Building | Home Wi-Fi |
| MAN | City | ISP Network |
| WAN | Global | Internet |
